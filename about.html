Lessons I Am Learning

Freshman year has taught me that talent alone is not enough at the college level. Every athlete was a standout in high school, which means effort and consistency matter even more now. I am learning how to handle pressure, accept constructive criticism, and stay confident even when I struggle. Practices are faster and more intense than anything I experienced before. The competition pushes me daily, and that pressure has helped me develop mental toughness.

Growth happens through discomfort. There are days when balancing assignments and athletic commitments feels overwhelming. However, those difficult moments are teaching me independence and responsibility. I have learned to plan my week carefully, communicate early, and hold myself accountable. Freshman year is not just about adjusting — it is about evolving.

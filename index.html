My Freshman College Softball Journey

Starting From Day One

Becoming a college softball player has completely changed my daily routine and mindset. As a freshman, everything feels new. The pace of practice, the expectations from coaches, and the independence of college life. My days begin before sunrise with lifting sessions and continue through classes, team meetings, and long practices. Adjusting to this schedule has forced me to grow quickly in discipline and time management. There are moments when I feel confident and prepared, and others when I feel overwhelmed. Every experience is shaping me into a stronger athlete and student.

This journey is about more than softball. It is about learning responsibility, building resilience, and stepping outside of my comfort zone. Balancing academics and Division I athletics requires organization and mental toughness. I am learning how to prioritize, communicate with professors, and support my teammates. Freshman year is challenging, but it is also exciting. Each practice, class, and game is helping me grow into the person I want to become.

Daily Freshman Routine

  • 6:00 a.m. Lifting
  • Morning and afternoon classes
  • Team practice and conditioning
  • Study sessions and homework
